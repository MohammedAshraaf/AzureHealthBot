Mental Health Bot

Use our free, reliable and anonymous mental health bot to screen for Anxiety, Depression and ADHD, and receive recommendations on next steps and assistance with contacting a mental health professional.

Bot Features

Our mental health screening bot provides preliminary screening for Anxiety, Depression and ADHD in a safe, friendly and interactive environment and thereafter, provides recommendations on how to manage the illness and provides references to mental health services for specialised treatment.

Mental Health Screening

Our mental health bot currently screens only for Anxiety, Depression and ADHD.

Free Use

No worrying about trials and subscriptions. Our bot is free for everyone to use.

Privacy/Anonymity

Your privacy is our topmost concern. That is why our bot does not request or collect any personal information from you.

Recommendations

If diagnosed positive, our bot will provide recommendations to help you manage the mental health illness.

Credibility

All questions asked during screening are from valid and reliable questionnaires (such as PHQ-9, GAD-7 and ASRS) widely used in the healthcare industry. References to the scientific literature can be found in the Info section of this app.

Professional Help

Ultimately, using our bot is not a substitute for seeking professional help so if diagnosed positive, our bot will provide resources to help you contact a licensed mental health professional for more specialised treatment.

How it works

1. Take the Screening

Click or tap the Chat icon at the bottom right of your screen or the Chat Now button at the top of this page to start chatting with the bot and begin the screening.

2. Understand your results

Our bot will provide you with a score regarding if you have Anxiety, Depression or ADHD. If your results return positive for any of these three illnesses, our bot will also let you know the severity level of the illness.

3. Follow our recommendations

Depending on your screening results, our bot will provide recommendations to help you manage the mental health illness.

4. Seek Professional Help

Ultimately, using our bot is not a substitute for seeking professional help. Hence, depending on your results, our bot will provide resources to help you contact a licensed mental health professional for more specialised treatment.

Info on Applicable Mental Health Illnesses

Our mental health screening bot currently only screens for Anxiety, Depression and ADHD. The results of your screening will let you know which of these conditions is responsible for your symptoms, and our bot will also provide recommendations and resources to help you manage the illness and get professional help.

  • How reliable are the questions asked during screening?

    All questions asked during screening are from valid and reliable questionnaires widely used in the healthcare industry. The scientific literature to validate these claims can be viewed below:

    1. PHQ-9 Questionnaire for Depression: Kroenke K, Spitzer RL, Williams JB (September 2001). "The PHQ-9: validity of a brief depression severity measure". Journal of General Internal Medicine. 16 (9): 606–13. doi:10.1046/j.1525-1497.2001.016009606.x
    2. GAD-7 Questionnaire for Anxiety: Spitzer RL, Kroenke K, Williams JB, Löwe B (May 2006). "A brief measure for assessing generalized anxiety disorder: the GAD-7". Archives of Internal Medicine. 166 (10): 1092–7. doi:10.1001/archinte.166.10.1092
    3. ASRS Questionnaire for ADHD: Adler, Lenard A.; Spencer, Thomas; Faraone, Stephen V.; Kessler, Ronald C.; Howes, Mary J.; Biederman, Joseph; Secnik, Kristina (July 2006). "Validity of Pilot Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale (ASRS) to Rate Adult ADHD Symptoms". Annals of Clinical Psychiatry. 18 (3): 145–148. doi:10.1080/10401230600801077
